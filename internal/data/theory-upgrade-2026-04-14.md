## 一、SEP 哲学理论搜索结果

### 1. Consciousness (SEP)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/consciousness/
- **摘要**: Consciousness is arguably the central issue in current theorizing about the mind. The SEP entry covers the history from Descartes through modern debates, the hard problem, representational theories, and higher-order approaches.

### 2. Qualia (SEP)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/qualia/
- **摘要**: Qualia are the subjective, phenomenal qualities of experience. The entry traces the term from Peirce (1866) through C.I. Lewis (1929) to contemporary debates on intrinsic, non-representational properties of experience.

### 3. Intentionality (SEP)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/intentionality/
- **摘要**: Intentionality is the power of minds to be about, represent, or stand for things and states of affairs. Introduced by Brentano, it lies at the interface of philosophy of mind and philosophy of language.

### 4. Self-Consciousness (SEP)
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/self-consciousness/
- **摘要**: Self-consciousness involves the capacity to be aware of oneself as oneself, central to personal identity and first-person authority.

### 5. Predictive Processing / Bayesian Brain
- **URL**: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/bayesian-epistemology/ (related)
- **摘要**: Predictive processing frameworks (Friston's free energy principle, Clark's predictive brain) model perception as Bayesian inference — the brain as a prediction machine minimizing prediction error.
